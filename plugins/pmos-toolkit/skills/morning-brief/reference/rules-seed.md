The skill categorizes every swept item into exactly one of these four buckets, using
the bullets here as its rubric. An item that matches no bullet is still placed by the
default sense of the taxonomy AND flagged `no-rule-matched` in the coverage manifest,
so a rule gap stays visible (it is never dropped — INV-2).

## do — needs an action from you, soon

- A direct ask or assignment addressed to you with a near-term deadline.
- A blocker where someone is waiting on your reply, decision, or sign-off.
- A calendar invite in the next day that needs your response.

## delegate-reply — a short reply or handoff clears it

- A question you can answer in a sentence or two.
- Something better owned by someone else — forward / delegate.
- A thread where a quick acknowledgement unblocks others.

## defer-track — real, but not today

- Work that matters but has no deadline pressure this morning.
- Items to convert into a tracked task (via /mytasks) rather than act on now.
- Scheduled events later in the window worth being aware of.

## drop-FYI — awareness only, no action

- Newsletters, digests, announcements, automated notifications.
- CCs where you are not the owner and no response is expected.
- Anything you would archive without reading in full.
