Should /ideate add a prior-art research phase?

Ideate artifact · 2026-05-28 · idea-type: extend

TL;DR

Don't add research to Phase 2 (Expand). The skill's anti-pattern #1 names variant diversity as the load-bearing invariant; injecting prior art into generation is the single best way to collapse it (LLMs anchor hard on whatever's in context).

Do add research as an optional pass between Expand and Pressure-test. Opt-in via single-tap AskUserQuestion at the convergence prompt; surfaces 2–3 prior-art bullets that flow into Pressure-test's Assumption-map (the "is this already solved?" assumption) and populates the existing "Alternatives & prior art" section of the artifact.

Narrow answer to the user's question: yes, putting research before Expand will impact variant quality — in the wrong direction. Lower diversity, more clones of prior art. Move it after Expand to preserve diversity.

How Might We

How might we let /ideate users tap prior art (how others have solved similar problems) without biasing variant generation in Expand?

Target user & JTBD

When framing a fuzzy idea, the user wants grounded inputs on existing solutions so Expand produces non-obvious variants — not reinvented wheels, not anchored clones.

Hypothesis

If /ideate offers research after blind variant generation (as a convergence input, not a generation input), then variant diversity is preserved and finalist selection is better-informed — without trading the load-bearing Expand invariant for a speculative Expand-quality gain.

Idea variants considered

  1. Optional research sub-phase 1.5, gated by --research, off by default.
  2. Mandatory research for extend/fix only; skipped for new.
  3. Hidden inline research inside Phase 2 generation (finalist).
  4. Post-Expand research as "did we miss anything?" pass, flowing into Pressure-test.
  5. Separate /ideate --research-only resume mode after Phase 5.
  6. Replace second auto-picked technique with "Analogous Inspiration (researched)" under --research.
  7. Research + adversarial "what did the research miss?" prompt in same pass.
  8. Blind Expand first, then show research at convergence (finalist, winner).
  9. Research as a constraint ("generate 12 variants that are meaningfully different from these 3").
  10. Cap research to 3 short bullets, hard-budgeted to limit anchoring.
  11. Pull research from ~/.pmos/learnings.md + workstream only (no web fetch).
  12. Skip research; add a prior-art-check line to Phase 3 battery instead.

How it works (winning variant: #8)

  1. Phases 1–2 unchanged. Expand still produces 8–15 blind variants with no prior-art context.
  2. At the Phase 2 convergence prompt, add a third option to the existing AskUserQuestion: "Show me prior art for these N finalists before I pick." Default: skip.
  3. If opted in: run a WebSearch (or learnings-only fallback per #11) keyed off HMW + finalist labels. Cap: 3 bullets total, ≤120 chars each.
  4. Surface bullets inline in the chat; user then picks finalists.
  5. Research bullets are written into the artifact's Alternatives & prior art section (existing, currently underused).
  6. One assumption is auto-added to Phase 3 Assumption-map: "this idea is meaningfully different from <closest prior art>", with cheapest-test "5-min comparison read".

Alternatives & prior art

(Empty by design — this artifact is the prior-art exploration for the feature it proposes. Recursive but accurate: the dogfooded run shows the section is the right place to land prior art when the skill ships the new pass.)

  • How /superpowers:brainstorming handles prior art: doesn't — purely divergent, no research step.
  • How /creativity handles it: explicit "Analogous Inspiration" technique pulls cross-domain analogies but from model knowledge, not live research.
  • How /grill handles it: surfaces prior art via interrogator questions ("has anyone solved this?") but only against a committed plan.

Munger Inversion — to guarantee failure

Cross-cutting decision: #8 wins on all three axes — risk-density 1 vs 2; assumption-load 1 vs 3; ease-of-validation 4 vs 2.

Success signals

  • Inter-variant distance in Expand stays ≥ baseline after the research pass ships (no regression on the load-bearing invariant).
  • Opt-in rate at the convergence prompt stabilizes between 30–70% (any lower = checkbox; any higher = should be default).
  • ≥30% of opt-in sessions show finalist-pick change after seeing research (proves the step earns its turn).
  • Artifact's "Alternatives & prior art" section is populated with concrete entries in ≥80% of opt-in sessions (vs current ~0%).
  • No user reports of "/ideate variants got more generic" within 4 weeks of shipping.

Next steps

  1. Validate the cheapest assumption first: A/B 5 ideas with and without prior-art injection into Expand. If diversity doesn't drop, the whole concern is moot and #3 becomes viable. (~30 min.)
  2. If diversity drops as predicted: draft a tight spec for #8 — one new optional sub-phase 2.5, one flag (--research / --no-research), reuses WebSearch, writes into existing artifact section. /requirements ideate-research-phase.
  3. Grill before committing: /grill the proposed #8 against alternatives #11 (learnings-only, no web) and #12 (no new phase, prior-art line in Pressure-test) — those are cheaper still and may dominate.
  4. Consider /ideate --refine --resume on this artifact if it gets shared.

Open questions

  • Is variant quality actually a complaint, or are we solving a problem nobody has? (Highest-leverage question — answer before building anything.)
  • Should research source be web (WebSearch / context7) or learnings-only (~/.pmos/learnings.md + workstream)? Web is richer; learnings-only ships without new dependencies and has lower anchoring risk.
  • Should research auto-trigger for extend/fix types (where prior art is most relevant) and stay opt-in for new?
  • Does "Analogous Inspiration" technique (already supported in Phase 1) already cover this implicitly via model knowledge? If so, the gap is smaller than it looks.
