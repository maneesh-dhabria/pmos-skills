# Two-Sided Repair Marketplace — market entry strategy

> **Anonymized synthetic sample** — not a real product document. Authored for story 260624-fbd as a
> few-shot exemplar and eval fixture for `/artifact-critique`. Names, numbers, and products are invented.
> Exercises a **two-sided completeness** gap and a **wedge-vs-moat** pricing/strategy critique.

## Vision

Become the default way people book vetted home-repair professionals. We will win because our prices are
the lowest in the category and our app is the easiest to use.

## Why now

Home-repair booking is still mostly phone calls and word of mouth. A clean mobile experience can capture
demand that has nowhere good to go today.

## The plan

Launch in one metro, subsidize the first thousand bookings to seed demand, and grow city by city. Our
take rate of ten percent undercuts the incumbents' fifteen to twenty percent, so customers save money on
every job.

## Differentiation

We are cheaper and simpler than anyone else in the market. As we grow, our scale lets us push prices down
further, which brings in more customers.

## Target

Homeowners aged thirty to fifty in mid-size metros who are comfortable booking services on their phone and
value convenience over a long-standing relationship with a tradesperson.

## Risks

The main risk is that customer acquisition is more expensive than planned. We will watch CAC closely and
slow spending if it runs high.
