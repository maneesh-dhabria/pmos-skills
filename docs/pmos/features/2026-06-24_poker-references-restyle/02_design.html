Epic: 260624-3nj · Plugin: pmos-gamekit · Route: skill · Status: defining (define run 2026-06-24)

Lineage. An enhancement to the existing single-file /poker game (plugins/pmos-gamekit/skills/poker/game/poker.html, 1,152 lines — its engine was originally ported from the maintainer's poker-coach repo). Shaped during this define run from the maintainer's seed plus a first-hand study of the source app at /Users/maneeshdhabria/Desktop/Projects/personal/poker-coach (Next.js + React + zustand): the running dev server was played for several hands and every reference surface was screenshotted, and the codebase was mapped to the file level (core/charts/, core/mental/, components/RankingsTab.tsx, components/PreflopChartTab.tsx, components/table/). This page is the epic design_doc: — the coherence contract the single build story cites by anchor. 1. Problem & positioning

The maintainer's poker-coach app pairs a No-Limit Hold'em table with a right-hand panel of genuinely useful static learning references — a hand-ranking ladder, an interactive preflop opening chart, and a pot-odds / mental-math method. Those references are valuable on their own and worth having beside any casual game. The shipped /poker gamekit skill, by contrast, is pure play — no references at all, and its table, while already felt-green/gold, does not match the source app's polished oval-felt look.

What to take, what to leave. poker-coach also has heavy live coaching — per-decision verdicts (✅/⚠️/❌), a Monte-Carlo equity engine, live "chart says raise" hints, and a narrative Coaching tab. The maintainer explicitly does not want any of that: it makes a casual game feel like homework and drags in an equity engine, workers, and an analysis layer the gamekit charter has no room for. The goal is the references, not the coach.

2. Reference surface — the toggle-able side panel (D1)

The references live in a collapsible right-hand panel that mirrors poker-coach's layout: the table occupies the left, the panel the right, toggled by a References button in the HUD. This is the maintainer's chosen surface (over a full-screen overlay or a top tab-toggle) because it keeps references visible alongside play — the way the source app works.

  • Layout. A two-column flex shell: table (flex-grow) + aside.refs (fixed ~340px). The existing table's responsive scale()-to-fit logic absorbs the narrower column. Toggling the panel re-fits the table.
  • Collapse. The panel is open/closed by the HUD button and remembers its state for the session only (no persistence — D5). When closed, the table reclaims the full width.
  • Narrow viewports. Below ~880px the panel becomes a full-width overlay above the table (the source app collapses 2-col → 1-col at the same breakpoint), dismissed by the same button / Esc.
  • Tabs inside the panel. Three stacked reference sections — Hand rankings, Preflop chart, Pot odds — presented as a small tab strip (matching the source's tabs) or a single scroll; the build story picks whichever reads cleaner at 340px. No "Live Feedback" / "Coaching" tabs exist (dropped).

3. The three references (static — D3, D4)

Every reference is static: it is the same content regardless of the hand in play, and nothing is computed per-decision. The one concession to convenience (D3): the preflop chart may default its Position selector to the hero's current seat — a pure pre-selection of a browsable control, not advice — and is freely changeable.

3.2 Interactive preflop chart

A 13×13 grid of the 169 canonical starting hands (pairs on the diagonal, suited upper-right, offsuit lower-left), each cell a real button colored by recommended action (raise = green, fold = muted/red), with two selectors — Position (UTG / MP / CO / BTN / SB / BB) and Facing (first-in unopened / vs a raise). Clicking a cell shows a small detail line: the hand, its action, its win% vs a random hand, and the plain rationale. This replicates poker-coach's PreflopChartTab.tsx.

Data (D6). The chart binds three bundled, inline static data structures adapted from the maintainer's own poker-coach (their work — no third-party licensing):

  • open — per-position RFI (raise-first-in) ranges as arrays of 169-grid keys, for UTG / MP / CO / BTN / SB. Ranges widen by seat (UTG tightest ~27 hands; BTN widest). Source: core/charts/preflopCharts.json.
  • vsOpen.BB — the big-blind defend range, split into 3-bet (raise) and call buckets. Source: same JSON.
  • equity169 — precomputed win% vs one random hand for all 169 keys (e.g. AA 85.3, AKs 67.3, 22 50.4). Source: core/charts/preflopEquity.json.

Honesty caveat (preserved). The source labels these a "transparent baseline, not solver output." The chart carries the same one-line note, and shows an explanatory panel (never a fabricated all-fold grid) for the spots the baseline doesn't model — BB unopened, and any position vs-a-raise outside the defend range. This is the only place any "should-you-raise" claim appears, and it is framed as a reference chart, not live advice (so it does not reintroduce the dropped coaching).

3.3 Pot odds + Rule of 2 & 4

A static "how to do the math in your head" card — the method, not a live per-hand readout. Adapted from poker-coach's MentalMathSection.tsx + core/mental/, but reduced to a fixed explainer (the live six-step walkthrough is part of the dropped coaching):

  • Counting outs — what an out is; the common draws (flush draw = 9, open-ended straight = 8, gutshot = 4, overcards = ~6 soft).
  • Rule of 2 & 4 — outs × 4 ≈ chance to hit by the river (on the flop), outs × 2 ≈ chance to hit on the next card (turn); the big-draw caveat (×4 overcounts above ~12 outs).
  • Pot odds & break-even — break-even% = toCall / (pot + toCall), with a worked example and a small reference table (e.g. half-pot bet = call needs ~25%, pot-sized = ~33%).
  • Putting it together — compare your chance-to-hit to the price; call when your equity beats the break-even number.

This is presented as a compact illustrated card. No state, no per-hand numbers, no equity engine.

4. Table restyle (D2)

The chosen fidelity tier is references + match the table look — adopt poker-coach's table visuals without the full setup/parity overhaul. Targets, taken from the source's components/table/:

  • Oval felt with the hero anchored bottom-center, opponents arranged around the oval, and the pot/board in the vertical center. The existing fixed-design-box + transform: scale()-to-fit approach is retained (and is what lets the side panel coexist) — the build keeps a readability floor and a ResizeObserver re-fit.
  • Seat tiles showing stack, last action, an acting glow, a winner glow, and a per-street chip strip (P / F / T / R badges with the amount committed that street) — replicating the source seat.
  • Card faces — cream face, red hearts/diamonds vs near-black spades/clubs, green-gradient back, gold outline on winning cards.
  • Felt-green / gold palette reconciled with the source tokens (--felt, --gold, verdict colors are dropped). The existing game already uses a close palette, so this is a refinement, not a rewrite.
  • Action bar — fold / check / call / raise with the size slider + ½ / ¾ / Pot quick buttons and unit toggle (USD / BB), matching the source's ActionBar. The existing game already has most of this; align the styling.

Explicitly NOT in scope (D2): the setup screen's per-bot style/skill pickers and table presets, bankroll persistence across sessions, and the one-at-a-time bot-action reveal animation are not required (they belong to the "full parity" tier the maintainer did not pick). /poker keeps its existing simple start screen (opponent count) and its no-save rule (D5/D7-existing).

5. HUD title + pmos watermark (D7)

Two maintainer-requested HUD changes (added mid-define):

  • Title → "Poker". The in-game HUD/title currently reads "No-Limit Hold'em" / "No-Limit Texas Hold'em" in four places (<title>, the start <h1>, an engine comment, and a panel header). The user-facing HUD heading becomes simply Poker. (The skill's external name: / description and the launcher title stay as-is — see §7; this is a display-string change inside the game.)
  • pmos watermark. A small pmos wordmark in the HUD (corner / top strip) linking to the pmos-skills GitHub repo — https://github.com/maneesh-dhabria/pmos-skills — opening in a new tab (target="_blank" rel="noopener"). Mirrors the recent gamekit pattern (the /flappy-bird top-HUD pmos wordmark). Unobtrusive, does not overlap play controls, and is keyboard-focusable.

6. Invariants (unchanged contract)

  • Single self-contained HTML (game/poker.html) — zero dependencies, no CDN, no build step, opens offline from file://, launched through the shared _shared/game-launcher (D5). The references, chart data, and restyle are all inlined.
  • Pure, window-exposed, self-tested engine. PokerEngine stays DOM-free and exposed on window for the existing VM tests; tests/run.mjs stays green and is extended to cover any new pure reference helpers (e.g. the 169-key chart lookup chartAction(cards, position, facing) and handKey()) — those are pure data functions and belong in the selftest (§H: deterministic logic is gated by a script, not the model).
  • No coaching, no equity engine, no network, no persistence. Closing the tab discards the table; nothing is saved.

7. Story split — one story (D8)

Single story, no split. Every change lands in the one self-contained game/poker.html (plus its tests/run.mjs): the side panel, the three references, the chart data, the table restyle, and the HUD/watermark all share the same file and the same CSS/layout surface. Parallel story branches would conflict on essentially every section, and none of these pieces is independently shippable on its own (the panel needs the layout; the references need the panel; the restyle touches the same DOM). Per the D24 split litmus — if a task in one story would depend on a task in another, fix the split — this is one vertical slice. The epic is therefore a singleton: epic 260624-3nj → story 260624-f62, route: skill, plugin pmos-gamekit, no dependencies.

The skill's SKILL.md gets a small edit: the "pure play — no coaching or equity panel" line becomes "pure play plus a static References panel (hand rankings, preflop chart, pot-odds math)", and the launch-report text mentions the References toggle. The external name: and trigger phrases are unchanged.

9. Out of scope (v1)

  • Any live coaching: per-decision verdicts, equity bars, EV tables, "chart says raise" live hints, the narrative Coaching tab, the Monte-Carlo equity engine + worker.
  • Setup-screen parity: per-bot style/skill pickers, table presets, coaching-depth selector, instant-feedback toggle.
  • Bankroll / session persistence, hand history, save state.
  • One-at-a-time bot-action reveal animation and showdown narrative banners beyond the existing winner display (nice-to-have; not required at this tier).
  • A bot-style reference card (D4 — dropped; bot personas aren't exposed in /poker's start screen).
