Morning brief — trustworthy daily triage

Created using pmos‑toolkit

The shaped problem

Each morning — and after any away stretch — an experienced PM has no single picture of what arrived across his work sources (email, team chat, calendar, doc comments — whichever sources he declares) that he can trust. Three co-equal pains follow (confirmed 2026-07-02 follow-up: "roughly equal mix"): he re-sweeps every tool himself (a cold-start tax before real work begins); he risks a request slipping through and surfacing late as a broken commitment; and even items he catches don't reliably turn into tracked commitments — work discussed is not work captured. An item may only leave the picture by being resolved (dismissed or tracked), never merely by having been seen — "nothing is missed" spans runs, not just arrivals. The deepest sub-problem is not the sweep itself but staying trustworthy over time: judgment rules rot silently, sources break silently, and the moment the picture looks complete but isn't, it is worse than no picture at all — because by then he has stopped checking the sources.

Who / when / why-now: A solo experienced PM running his own AI-skill toolkit; every workday morning and after travel/meeting-heavy stretches. Why now: connectors (Gmail, Calendar, Drive, Notion) newly make a machine-side sweep of his real work sources possible, and /mytasks import already provides a confirm-gated path from assessed items to tracked tasks — the missing piece is a coverage picture he can keep believing.

This is a problem brief. It deliberately proposes no solution — see Handoff below.

Felt problem (HMW + JTBD)

How might we: How might an experienced PM start each workday trusting — without re-sweeping every tool himself — that everything that arrived across his work sources has been seen, judged consistently against his own rules, and, where it demands action, turned into a tracked commitment?

Who: A solo, senior product manager (the toolkit's own author) juggling email, team chat, calendar, and collaborative-doc comments.

When / where: Sitting down at the start of each workday; most acutely after any period away (travel, back-to-back meeting days, weekends).

Job-to-be-done: "When I sit down in the morning, I want to see everything that arrived and what each item needs from me, so I can commit to a plan for the day without the background fear that something slipped."

Falsifiable hypothesis: If we solve trustworthy cross-source coverage with durable personal judgment rules, then the manual all-tool morning sweep observably stops — the brief is opened every workday and the old tool-by-tool reconstruction isn't. Counter-evidence that falsifies it: the brief is read and the tools are still swept — trust failed and the artifact is pure overhead. (Grill 2026-07-02: measurement simplified from a self-logged miss diary — which wouldn't realistically be kept — to the adoption + sweep-stops signal, consistent with the no-numeric-bar stance.)

Abstraction ladder

Climbing why / descending how to find the right altitude — a 5-Whys was run because the seed arrived solution-shaped (it named the artifact, the connectors, and the target skill).

  • Rung 0 (the seed, solution altitude): "I need a /morning-brief skill that reads my connectors and drafts my day." — an artifact, not a problem.
  • Why? → I don't know what needs me today without opening four-plus tools one by one.
  • Why does that hurt? → Incoming requests, follow-ups, and comments live scattered across email / team chat / calendar / doc threads with no single trustworthy picture; each morning starts with reconstruction.
  • Why does the scatter matter? → Items get missed or handled late; the same item is re-read and re-judged every time it resurfaces; things I said "I'll follow up" on never become tracked tasks.
  • Why does missing things matter? → Broken commitments erode trust with colleagues and stakeholders (external cost) and keep mornings reactive and anxious (internal cost).
  • Rung above (too abstract): "How should a PM allocate scarce attention?" — a philosophy, not a shapeable unit; rejected as the working altitude.

Chosen altitude: the trustworthy-picture rung — converting scattered incoming communication into a reliable, low-effort picture of what needs me and when, that does not depend on my memory or on my manually sweeping every tool.

Decomposition

Sub-problems at the chosen altitude, with the real one marked ★.

  1. Coverage (the sweep): collecting everything that arrived across sources since the last look — windowing, and the hard fact that some named sources (team chat, Google-Docs comments) currently have no connector at all in this environment.
  2. Consistent judgment (the heuristics): deciding what each item is — action-needed / follow-up / waiting-on / FYI — the same way every time, from durable personal rules rather than morning-by-morning gut calls. Research caveat: hand-written rule systems rot silently and their maintenance burden compounds faster than their benefit.
  3. ★ Trust & verifiability (staying believed): the user must be able to keep believing the picture is complete — in week 4, not just day 1. A false negative (a missed item) destroys trust far faster than noise does; silent breakage of a rule or a source is the canonical killer. If trust fails, the user re-sweeps all tools anyway and every other sub-problem's solution becomes pure overhead. This is the make-or-break sub-problem precisely because everything else has an existing partial answer (see 4–5) while this one has none.
  4. Translation (comms → commitments): assessed items becoming tracked tasks. Largely pre-solved substrate: /mytasks import already implements a parse → preview → confirm-before-write → report flow; the gap is only the hand-off of an assessment into it.
  5. Planning (the day itself): composing today's calendar + open tasks into a plan. Partially served today by /mytasks today; downstream of a trusted picture, not the core.

Chosen framing (confirmed with the user, 2026-07-02 follow-up): (a) trustworthy coverage is the organizing frame, but the three confirmed co-equal pains make triage speed and capture-into-commitments first-class outcomes, not folded-in clauses — an item is only "handled" once it is either consciously dismissed or tracked, and the morning picture must be cheap to clear, not just complete. Additionally confirmed: the complete morning picture includes a read-only "already committed" lane derived from /mytasks (due today, overdue, check-ins due, waiting-on), with a dedupe rule — an incoming item already tracked as a task appears once, in the tasks lane. /mytasks remains the sole system of record; the full ordered-day-plan framing (b) stays rejected.

Grill hardening (2026-07-02, deep, 8 questions — report: .pmos/grills/2026-07-02_morning-brief-skill.html): the chosen framing survived interrogation with four refinements now binding on downstream work:

  • Show everything, rank it. Recall is structural — every in-window item appears at least as a one-line FYI row; heuristics decide prominence, never inclusion. A judgment miss becomes "ranked too low", which is detectable; the manifest alone could only prove source-level coverage. Volume is absorbed by prominence tiers with FYI collapsed to expandable counts — completeness lives in the layers, the daily read is the top.
  • Derive, don't store. The brief is a pure view over source state + /mytasks — no per-item triage ledger. "Dismissing" an item is a confirm-gated source-native action (archive the email, resolve the comment): the source is the state store, and clearing the brief literally clears the inboxes. Only a last-run cursor timestamp persists. Sources with no dismissal affordance stay visible at low prominence.
  • Batch review, one confirm. Each run proposes one editable action set (create these tasks / archive these / leave these) confirmed once — not per-item approvals, which manufacture the rubber-stamping reflex Lens 5 names. Matches /mytasks import's preview→confirm shape.
  • User-indicated priority, never assumed. Extends the source abstraction from presence to importance: declared sources and their relative weight are user configuration; the skill assumes nothing about any user's channel mix.

Open questions

All eight original open questions were resolved with the user in an interactive follow-up on 2026-07-02 (plus one new question the user raised, and one the user asked back). Resolutions:

  • 1. Pain ranking → Roughly equal mix. Fear-of-missing, triage time-cost, and the discussed-but-never-tracked gap are co-equal, all first-class; coverage stays the organizing frame.
  • 2 & 3. Source reach → reframed as a generic abstraction. Sources are user-declared, not hardcoded; the skill assumes a declared source's connector is present, and the coverage manifest surfaces per-run gaps (a declared source that couldn't be reached). Slack/GDocs-comments become per-user capability facts, not skill-level exclusions.
  • 4. Run model → on-demand, in a live session (connectors authed, brief fresh). Retires the headless-auth dependency.
  • 4b. Windowing (user's question back) → new + still-open, derived. Two lanes: everything new since the last run (cursor), plus carryover — items still unresolved in their source (unarchived, unreplied, unresolved comment) and not yet tracked. An item leaves the brief only by being resolved, never by being seen. Refined at grill: carryover is derived from source state each run, not from a stored per-item assessment ledger; dismissal is a source-native action (see Grill hardening in Competing framings).
  • 5. Data residency → ~/.pmos/ home-directory residence; work content never lives in any repo.
  • 6. Week-4 trust → coverage manifest per run (receipts: sources swept, counts, sources NOT reached, no-heuristic-matched items surfaced).
  • 7. Plugin home → pmos-toolkit by adjacency to /mytasks//people.
  • 8. /magazine boundary → confirmed by default (not explicitly asked; user did not object): private actionable work comms here, public learning content stays /magazine.
  • 9. Existing-tasks lane (user's new question) → yes, read-only. The brief includes an "already committed" lane derived from /mytasks (due today, overdue, check-ins, waiting-on) with a dedupe rule (a tracked incoming item appears once, in the tasks lane); /mytasks remains the sole system of record. The full ordered-day-plan framing stays rejected.
  • Heuristics lifecycle (asked alongside) → observe + correct, cold start. Start from a small generic taxonomy (≈GTD 4D); every correction at the confirm step becomes a candidate rule; rules are earned from real traffic so decay stays visible. Grill re-tested the cold start against the 3-week adoption window and it stands, with recorded rationale: mis-seeded priors (e.g., from /people) are harder to notice and unlearn than no priors.
  • Speed bar (grill) → no numeric target. "Cheap to clear" stays a felt quality with adoption as its proxy; a numeric clear-time bar was considered and deliberately declined.
  • Auto-picks from the earlier AFK stretch (now superseded by the follow-up): slug on Recommended after prompt timeout; context bucket applied silently from persisted signal; autonomous shaping path used; framing convergence recorded as assumption — since confirmed/refined above.
