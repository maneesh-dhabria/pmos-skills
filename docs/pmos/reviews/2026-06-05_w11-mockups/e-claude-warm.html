pmos 02 — Spec: Inline doc comments

Specification · Tier 3

Inline doc comments

Stakeholders annotate any PMOS-emitted HTML artifact in the browser. Comment threads persist as an inline JSON block inside the HTML itself — no sidecar files; the artifact is the single source of truth. This mockup shows Direction E — Claude Warm applied to a representative spec page.

Context & goals

A warm, humanist, paper-like feel in the Claude/Anthropic idiom: a soft cream canvas, the warm terracotta accent, and a calm transitional serif (Newsreader) for every heading and the wordmark. Body stays on a clean sans for readability. The section signature is a small terracotta §N eyebrow over a hairline rule — quiet and editorial.

Warmth reads as care. A cream page and a humanist serif make a spec feel considered, not generated.

Functional requirements

FR-1 — Masthead wordmark

The toolbar carries pmos in Newsreader, terracotta — literary and warm, the opposite of a sterile export. It appears on all 14 HTML surfaces.

FR-2 — Section signature

Each <h2> sits under a hairline rule with a small terracotta §N eyebrow above it. Restrained, repeats gracefully down a long document.
